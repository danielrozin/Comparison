# Christmas Cookie Recipes: 8 Classics That Come Out Right Every Time

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | November 19, 2026*

The most reliable Christmas cookie is the one with the fewest variables. That means using room-temperature butter (cold butter doesn't cream properly), measuring flour by spooning it into the cup rather than scooping (scooping compacts the flour and adds up to 20% more), and baking on the middle rack of your oven. Get those three things right and the recipe does the rest. The eight cookies below span the classics — cut-out sugar cookies through no-bake chocolate oats — with specific techniques called out wherever home bakers most commonly go wrong.

## Equipment You'll Need

Most Christmas cookie recipes share the same equipment list:
- Stand mixer or hand mixer with paddle attachment
- Rimmed baking sheets (light-colored aluminum holds heat more evenly than dark nonstick)
- Parchment paper (prevents sticking without greasing)
- Cooling rack
- A kitchen scale for weighing flour (optional but improves consistency significantly)

## 1. Classic Cut-Out Sugar Cookies

The most popular Christmas cookie by volume, and the most forgiving once you understand one thing: the dough must be properly chilled before cutting.

**Ingredients (makes ~48 2-inch cookies):**
- 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
- 1 large egg
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp almond extract (optional but traditional)

**Method:**
1. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside.
2. Beat butter and sugar in a stand mixer on medium-high for 3 full minutes until pale and fluffy. Don't rush this — under-creamed butter produces flat, dense cookies.
3. Add egg and extracts; beat 30 seconds until combined.
4. Add the flour mixture all at once and mix on low until just combined. The dough will look crumbly at first; keep mixing until it holds together.
5. Divide dough in half, flatten each into a disc, and wrap in plastic. Refrigerate at least 1 hour, or overnight.
6. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll one disc of dough to ¼-inch thickness on a lightly floured surface.
7. Cut shapes and place 1 inch apart on parchment-lined baking sheets.
8. Bake 8–10 minutes, until edges are just set and centers look slightly underdone. They firm up as they cool.
9. Cool completely before decorating.

**Butter note:** [Unsalted butter vs. salted butter](/compare/salted-vs-unsalted-butter) in cookies comes down to control. Unsalted lets you adjust the salt yourself; salted works but you'll want to reduce the added salt in the recipe by half. Either produces a good cookie — just be consistent.

**Royal Icing for Decorating:**
- 4 cups (480g) powdered sugar, sifted
- 3 tablespoons meringue powder
- 6 tablespoons warm water
- Gel food coloring

Whisk until smooth and stiff (for outlining), then thin with water 1 tablespoon at a time for flood consistency (filling in areas). This icing dries hard within 2 hours at room temperature.

## 2. Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread cuts sharply, holds detail in molds, and smells like the holidays. The molasses is the flavor backbone — don't reduce it.

**Ingredients (makes ~36 medium cookies):**
- 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
- 1½ tsp baking soda
- 1 tbsp ground ginger
- 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp ground cloves
- ½ tsp salt
- ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter, room temperature
- ¾ cup (150g) firmly packed dark brown sugar
- 1 large egg
- ½ cup (170g) unsulfured molasses

**Method:**
1. Whisk all dry ingredients together. Set aside.
2. Beat butter and brown sugar until fluffy, about 2 minutes. Add egg and molasses; mix until combined.
3. Add dry ingredients and mix on low until a cohesive dough forms.
4. Chill dough at least 2 hours.
5. Roll to ¼ inch thickness and cut shapes. Place on parchment-lined sheets.
6. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8–10 minutes. Edges should look set; centers will firm as they cool.

The [baking soda vs. baking powder](/compare/baking-soda-vs-baking-powder) question comes up here because gingerbread uses baking soda, not baking powder. The reason: baking soda reacts with the acid in molasses, creating a more even lift and deeper brown color. Substituting baking powder would require using three times the amount and would result in a paler cookie with a slightly bitter aftertaste.

## 3. Snickerdoodles

A cream of tartar cookie with a cinnamon-sugar crust. The tanginess from cream of tartar is the defining characteristic — don't skip it or substitute.

**Ingredients (makes ~36 cookies):**
- 2¾ cups (330g) all-purpose flour
- 2 tsp cream of tartar
- 1 tsp baking soda
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1½ cups (300g) sugar, divided
- 2 large eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 tbsp ground cinnamon

**Method:**
1. Whisk flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt.
2. Beat butter and 1¼ cups sugar until fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla; mix well.
3. Stir in flour mixture until combined.
4. Refrigerate 30 minutes.
5. Mix remaining ¼ cup sugar with cinnamon in a shallow bowl.
6. Roll dough into 1½-inch balls, then roll each in cinnamon sugar.
7. Place 2 inches apart on parchment-lined baking sheet.
8. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10–12 minutes. They should look slightly underdone — they finish cooking on the pan.

## 4. Thumbprint Cookies with Jam

A simple butter cookie pressed with a thumb and filled with jam or chocolate. The dough is forgiving; the main variable is the jam.

**Ingredients (makes ~30 cookies):**
- 2 cups (240g) all-purpose flour
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, room temperature
- ⅔ cup (130g) sugar
- 1 large egg yolk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- ⅓ cup jam or preserves (raspberry and apricot are traditional)

**Method:**
1. Whisk flour and salt. Beat butter and sugar until fluffy; add yolk and vanilla.
2. Stir in flour until combined. Chill 30 minutes.
3. Roll into 1-inch balls. Place on parchment-lined sheet.
4. Using your thumb or a ½ tsp measuring spoon, press an indentation into each ball.
5. Fill each indentation with ½ tsp jam.
6. Bake at 325°F (165°C) for 14–16 minutes until edges are lightly golden.

**Tip:** For cleaner indentations that don't puff shut, press them again halfway through baking and re-fill after baking if the jam bubbled out.

## 5. Pecan Sandies

A crumbly, buttery shortbread with toasted pecans. These freeze exceptionally well — a good candidate for baking early in the season.

**Ingredients (makes ~24 cookies):**
- 1½ cups (180g) all-purpose flour
- ½ cup (50g) powdered sugar
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 cup (100g) pecans, roughly chopped and toasted

**Method:**
1. Toast pecans at 325°F for 7 minutes until fragrant. Cool.
2. Pulse flour, sugar, and salt in a food processor. Add butter and pulse until pea-sized crumbs form.
3. Add vanilla and half the pecans; pulse until dough comes together.
4. Press remaining pecans into the dough by hand.
5. Shape into a 1½-inch diameter log, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate 1 hour.
6. Slice into ¼-inch rounds. Bake at 325°F for 14–18 minutes until barely golden.

## 6. Peanut Butter Blossoms

A classic with a Hershey's Kiss pressed into each center. The peanut butter cookie base comes out softer if you use creamy peanut butter, not natural-style (natural peanut butter's oil separation makes the dough harder to work with).

**Ingredients (makes ~48 cookies):**
- 1¾ cups (210g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- ½ tsp salt
- ½ cup (113g) butter, room temperature
- ½ cup (130g) creamy peanut butter
- ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar + more for rolling
- ½ cup (110g) packed brown sugar
- 1 large egg
- 2 tbsp milk
- 1 tsp vanilla
- 48 Hershey's Kisses, unwrapped

**Method:**
1. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt.
2. Beat butter, peanut butter, and both sugars until fluffy. Add egg, milk, and vanilla.
3. Stir in flour mixture.
4. Shape into 1-inch balls. Roll each in granulated sugar.
5. Bake at 375°F for 8–10 minutes.
6. Immediately press one Hershey's Kiss into each cookie as they come out of the oven. The cookie will crack around the edges — that's correct.

## 7. Spritz Cookies

A classic piped cookie made with a cookie press. The dough must be room temperature — slightly warm, even — for the press to work properly.

**Ingredients (makes ~72 small cookies):**
- 2½ cups (300g) all-purpose flour
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter, softened (not just room temperature — slightly warm)
- ¾ cup (150g) granulated sugar
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- ½ tsp almond extract

**Method:**
1. Whisk flour and salt.
2. Beat butter and sugar until very fluffy, 3 minutes. Add yolk and extracts.
3. Add flour and mix until combined.
4. Load into cookie press with desired disc. Press onto ungreased, room-temperature baking sheets — the butter must catch on a cool, un-greased surface.
5. Decorate with sprinkles or colored sugar.
6. Bake at 375°F for 10–12 minutes until edges are lightly golden.

## 8. No-Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies

No oven required. These are fudgy, set at room temperature, and take about 20 minutes total. Good for high-altitude kitchens where oven-baked cookies behave unpredictably.

**Ingredients (makes ~36 cookies):**
- 2 cups (400g) sugar
- ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter
- ½ cup (120ml) whole milk
- 4 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
- ½ tsp salt
- 3 cups (270g) quick oats (not rolled oats — quick oats absorb the liquid faster)
- ½ cup (130g) peanut butter
- 1 tsp vanilla

**Method:**
1. In a medium saucepan, combine sugar, butter, milk, cocoa, and salt. Bring to a full rolling boil while stirring. Once it reaches a boil, cook exactly 60 seconds — not less, not more. Under-cooking produces runny cookies; over-cooking produces crumbly ones.
2. Remove from heat. Stir in oats, peanut butter, and vanilla quickly.
3. Drop by tablespoon onto parchment paper. Allow to set at room temperature for 30 minutes.

## Freezing and Make-Ahead Strategy

Most Christmas cookie doughs freeze raw for up to 3 months:
- **Slice-and-bake logs** (sandies, shortbreads): freeze the log; slice and bake frozen, adding 2–3 extra minutes.
- **Drop cookies** (oatmeal, peanut butter): portion and freeze on a sheet; transfer to a bag; bake from frozen, adding 4–5 minutes.
- **Cut-out cookies**: freeze cut shapes before baking on a sheet; bake from frozen directly. Decorate after baking and cooling.

Baked cookies without icing also freeze well for up to 6 weeks in an airtight container with layers separated by parchment paper.
