# How to Make an Invoice: Required Fields, Free Templates, and Getting Paid Faster

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | July 24, 2026*

An invoice is a legally binding payment request — a document that says "I delivered this, on this date, for this price, and payment is due by this date." Getting it right matters for getting paid and for keeping clean records at tax time. Here's exactly what to include, how to format it, and what to avoid.

## What Every Invoice Must Include

Regardless of whether you use software, a Word template, or a Google Doc, a proper invoice needs these fields:

### 1. The Word "INVOICE" and an Invoice Number

Put "INVOICE" prominently at the top. This makes it unambiguous to the recipient and to their accounts payable team.

**Invoice numbers** should be sequential (INV-001, INV-002…) or date-based (INV-2026-07-001). They serve three purposes:
- Tracking which invoices are paid vs. outstanding
- Referencing in payment communications ("re: INV-023")
- Tax record-keeping

Never reuse an invoice number. If an invoice is cancelled, skip that number.

### 2. Your Business Name and Contact Information

Include:
- Your full legal name or business name
- Business address (or city/state if you work remotely)
- Email address
- Phone number (optional but helpful)
- Website (optional)

If you operate as an LLC or corporation, use the legal business name exactly as it appears on your formation documents.

### 3. Client's Name and Address

The "Bill To" section. Use the company name and the name of the specific person or department responsible for payment. For larger companies, addressing invoices to "Accounts Payable" or the finance team gets them processed faster than sending to your main contact.

### 4. Invoice Date

The date you created and sent the invoice. This is the reference date for calculating when payment is due.

### 5. Payment Due Date

Be explicit. Don't write "Net 30" unless you've confirmed your client understands what that means. Write the actual date: **Due: August 14, 2026**.

Common payment terms:
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Due on receipt | Payment expected immediately |
| Net 7 | Payment due 7 days after invoice date |
| Net 15 | Payment due 15 days after invoice date |
| Net 30 | Payment due 30 days after invoice date |
| Net 60 | Payment due 60 days after invoice date |

For freelancers and small businesses, Net 15 or Net 30 is standard. Net 7 is appropriate for smaller jobs or clients with a history of slow payment.

### 6. Itemized List of Services or Products

This is the core of the invoice. List each item, service, or deliverable separately with:
- Description (specific enough that there's no confusion)
- Quantity (hours, units, or "1 for flat-fee items")
- Rate (per hour, per unit, or flat fee)
- Line total (quantity × rate)

**Example:**

| Description | Qty | Rate | Total |
|-------------|-----|------|-------|
| Website redesign — homepage and 3 interior pages | 1 | $2,500.00 | $2,500.00 |
| Logo refresh (2 rounds of revisions) | 1 | $800.00 | $800.00 |
| Rush fee — delivered 5 days ahead of original deadline | 1 | $350.00 | $350.00 |
| **Subtotal** | | | **$3,650.00** |
| Sales tax (8.25% — Texas) | | | $301.13 |
| **Total Due** | | | **$3,951.13** |

Be specific in descriptions. "Web work" invites disputes. "Homepage redesign including mobile breakpoints and copywriting integration, per scope document dated June 15, 2026" does not.

### 7. Subtotal, Taxes, and Total

Show:
- Subtotal (before tax)
- Any applicable sales tax (varies by state/country, service type, and whether you're invoicing a business or consumer)
- Discounts (if any)
- Grand total in large, clear type

**On sales tax:** Services are taxable in some states and not others. Software-as-a-service (SaaS) has different rules than physical goods. If you're unsure whether to charge sales tax, consult an accountant — charging incorrect tax creates liability.

### 8. Payment Methods and Instructions

Tell the client exactly how to pay:
- **Bank transfer (ACH):** Include your bank name, routing number, and account number. Many clients prefer this; it's free and fast.
- **Check:** "Payable to [Your Business Name]" and your mailing address
- **PayPal/Venmo:** Your username or linked email
- **Credit card:** If you accept cards, include the payment link (e.g., via Stripe, Wave, Square)
- **Wire transfer:** Include SWIFT code for international clients

### 9. Notes and Terms (Optional but Useful)

A short notes section can include:
- Late payment fees ("A 1.5% monthly fee applies to balances 30+ days past due")
- Project-specific notes
- Thank-you message ("Thank you for your business")
- References to the original contract or scope of work

Late fee clauses are enforceable in most US states when clearly stated on the invoice and agreed to upfront (ideally in your original contract).

## Free Invoice Templates

You don't need special software. These work immediately:

**Google Docs / Google Sheets:**
- Search "invoice template" in Google Docs' template gallery
- Free, shareable, accessible from anywhere
- Customizable in 5 minutes

**Microsoft Word / Excel:**
- Invoice templates built into both Office apps
- File → New → search "invoice"
- Download once, reuse forever

**Wave (Free Invoice Software):**
- wave.com — completely free invoicing platform
- Sends invoices, tracks payment status, integrates with Wave's free accounting
- Best free option for freelancers who want something purpose-built

**FreshBooks / QuickBooks / Bonsai:**
- Paid platforms ($10–$30/month) with invoicing, time tracking, and accounting combined
- Worth the cost once you're billing $3,000+/month

## How to Send an Invoice

**Email** is standard. Send as a PDF — not a link, not a Word doc, not in the body of the email. PDF prevents edits, looks professional, and renders consistently on any device.

Email subject line format that works: `Invoice #INV-042 — [Client Name] — Due [Date]`

Body (keep it short):
> Hi [Name],
>
> Please find attached Invoice #INV-042 for [brief description], totaling $X,XXX.XX, due [date].
>
> [Payment instructions or link]
>
> Let me know if you have any questions.
>
> [Your name]

Send the invoice the same day you deliver work. Delaying your invoice signals that payment urgency is low.

## Following Up on Late Invoices

If a payment is 1–3 days past due:
- Send a brief, friendly reminder email with the invoice attached
- Assume it's an oversight

If 7+ days past due:
- Email and call if possible
- Reference the specific invoice number and amount
- Remind them of your late fee policy

If 30+ days past due:
- Send a formal overdue notice
- Put future work on hold until payment is received
- Consider a collections service (they typically take 25–50% of recovered amounts) or small claims court for amounts under $10,000

**Prevention is better than chasing:** Require a deposit (25–50%) upfront for large projects. This screens out non-paying clients and ensures partial payment before you deliver the full work.

## Common Invoice Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Vague line item descriptions | Opens disputes about what was delivered | Be specific — reference scope document, dates, deliverables |
| No due date | "Payment due when convenient" means never | Always state a specific calendar date |
| Wrong legal name | Client's system can't process it | Confirm billing name/address before sending |
| Sending to the wrong person | Gets buried or forwarded slowly | Ask upfront who handles AP; send to that address |
| Not following up | Unpaid invoices stay unpaid | Set a reminder to follow up at due date |
| Charging wrong tax rate | Creates liability | Verify or ask an accountant |
| No payment instructions | Client has to ask how to pay | Include method + details in every invoice |

## Invoice Checklist

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Invoice number assigned (sequential)
- [ ] Invoice date included
- [ ] Payment due date specified (actual date, not just "Net 30")
- [ ] Your name/business and contact info
- [ ] Client's legal name and billing address
- [ ] Every line item listed with description, quantity, rate, and total
- [ ] Subtotal, taxes, and grand total shown
- [ ] Payment method and instructions clear
- [ ] Late fee policy included (if applicable)
- [ ] Saved as PDF before sending

A professional invoice takes 10 minutes to create and avoids weeks of payment confusion. Get the structure right once, save it as your template, and reuse it for every client.
