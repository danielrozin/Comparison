# Is the PS5 Pro Worth Buying in 2026? Complete Review vs Xbox Series X

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | June 8, 2027*

Sony released the PS5 Pro in November 2024 at $699 — $200 more than the standard PS5 Disc Edition and $250 more than the Digital Edition. After more than a year on the market, the question has become clearer: is the upgrade meaningful enough to justify the price?

The answer depends on what you want from a console and what you currently own.

---

## PS5 Pro: What's Actually Better

### The GPU Upgrade

The PS5 Pro's core upgrade is its GPU — approximately 45% more compute units than the standard PS5, with support for AMD's RDNA 3 architecture features including enhanced ray tracing and Mesh Shading. In practice, this means:

**Higher resolution at target frame rates:** More games hit 4K/60fps on the PS5 Pro compared to the standard PS5, which often had to choose between resolution (4K/30fps) or frame rate (1080p/60fps or 1440p/60fps). The Pro's extra GPU headroom resolves this tradeoff in many games.

**PlayStation Spectral Super Resolution (PSSR):** Sony's proprietary AI upscaling technology is the PS5 Pro's most impressive feature. PSSR performs comparably to Nvidia's DLSS — it takes a lower-resolution internal render and upscales it to near-native 4K quality with sharper edges and better detail than the older checkerboard rendering used on standard PS5. Games with PSSR patches look genuinely better than their standard PS5 counterparts.

**Ray tracing:** The PS5 Pro enables ray tracing at higher frame rates in supported games. On standard PS5, many games had to disable ray tracing entirely in performance mode to hit 60fps. The Pro can run some games with ray tracing at 60fps — a meaningful visual improvement in lighting, reflections, and shadows.

### PS5 Pro Enhancement Patches

The "PS5 Pro Enhanced" label appears on games that have received developer patches specifically targeting the Pro hardware. By early 2026, over 100 major titles have received Pro patches including:

- Spider-Man 2 (60fps with ray tracing)
- Gran Turismo 7 (8K support in replay mode)
- Final Fantasy VII Rebirth (60fps in quality mode + higher resolution)
- Horizon Forbidden West (4K/60fps with ray tracing)
- Demon's Souls (native 4K/60fps)
- Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart (60fps ray tracing)

Not every game has been patched, and the improvement varies significantly between titles.

### What's Missing on the PS5 Pro

At $699, the PS5 Pro omits some things that are surprising given the price:

- **No disc drive included** (add-on for $79.99)
- **No DualSense controller in the box** (comes with one)
- **No vertical stand included** (sold separately for $29.99)
- **No new controller** (same DualSense as standard PS5)

The disc drive and stand omissions are the most common complaints. Budget an additional $80-$110 if you want optical disc support and a stand.

## Xbox Series X: The Strongest Competitor

### Hardware Specs

The Xbox Series X launched in 2020 with specs that still compete well in 2026:

| Feature | Xbox Series X |
|---------|--------------|
| CPU | 8-core custom AMD Zen 2, 3.8GHz |
| GPU | 12 teraflops RDNA 2 |
| RAM | 16GB GDDR6 |
| Storage | 1TB NVMe SSD |
| Max Resolution | 8K capable, primarily 4K/120fps |
| Price | $499 |

The Series X is $200 cheaper than the PS5 Pro and still delivers excellent gaming experiences. The 4K/120fps capability is real in supported games (with appropriate TV/monitor), and the overall hardware is genuinely powerful.

### Xbox Game Pass

Xbox's strongest argument in 2026 isn't hardware — it's Game Pass Ultimate. At $19.99/month, Game Pass includes:

- 100+ first-party Xbox and Bethesda titles at launch
- EA Play library
- Cloud gaming (stream to phone/TV/PC without a console)
- PC Game Pass
- Select EA Play Pro games

For players who buy many games, Game Pass Ultimate can pay for itself quickly. The value proposition is strongest for players who sample many titles rather than replaying the same game repeatedly.

### First-Party Exclusives

Xbox's exclusive library is a persistent weakness relative to PlayStation. While Xbox owns major franchises — Halo, Forza, Gears, and the Bethesda catalog including Elder Scrolls, Fallout, Starfield — the volume and critical reception of exclusives hasn't matched PlayStation's output since 2020. Most Xbox exclusives also launch on PC, which reduces the console-only argument.

## Is the PS5 Pro Worth It?

### Yes, if:

- **You own a 4K display with a large screen (55"+):** The visual improvements from PSSR and higher-resolution rendering are most visible on large 4K screens. On a 1080p TV or small monitor, the Pro upgrade is nearly imperceptible.

- **You play Sony exclusives heavily:** God of War Ragnarök, Spider-Man 2, Final Fantasy VII Rebirth, Gran Turismo 7 — all look noticeably better on Pro with enhanced patches. If these games are your library, the upgrade has obvious value.

- **You want ray tracing at 60fps:** This is the technical advantage that matters most. If you've been playing games in "Performance Mode" (60fps, lower resolution, no RT) specifically because the standard PS5 couldn't hold 60fps with ray tracing, the Pro addresses that.

- **You already have a PS5 Disc Edition:** You can transfer your disc drive. The de facto upgrade cost is $699 vs selling your standard PS5 (typically $300-$350 in 2026), netting ~$350 additional spend.

### No, if:

- **You don't have a 4K TV:** Upgrade the display first.
- **You primarily play third-party games that haven't received Pro patches:** The improvement is marginal without patch support.
- **$699 is a stretch:** The standard PS5 at $449 (Disc) or $449 (Digital) plays every PS5 game. The Pro is an enthusiast purchase.
- **You're choosing between PS5 Pro and your first console ever:** At the same $699 budget, an Xbox Series X + 3 months of Game Pass Ultimate is a strong alternative if exclusives don't pull you to PlayStation.

---

## The Verdict

**The PS5 Pro is the best console available in 2026 for pure gaming performance** — PSSR upscaling, better ray tracing, and the PlayStation exclusive library make it the premium choice for enthusiasts who play on 4K displays.

**Xbox Series X at $499 is the better value** for budget-conscious players, fans of Xbox exclusives, or anyone who'd rather have the included disc drive and put the $200 savings toward games or Game Pass.

**The standard PS5 is the sleeper pick** — at $449, it plays every PS5 game identically to the Pro for non-Pro-patched titles and is $250 cheaper.
