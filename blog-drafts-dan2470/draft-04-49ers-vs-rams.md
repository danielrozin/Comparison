# 49ers vs Rams 2026: Rivalry History, Head-to-Head Stats & Key Matchups

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | July 19, 2026*

The San Francisco 49ers vs Los Angeles Rams is one of the NFC West's defining rivalries — two franchises that have shared the same division since the Rams moved back to Los Angeles in 2016, and two teams that meet twice a season with playoff seeding often on the line. In recent years, this matchup has become one of the most competitive and consequential in the NFC.

---

## All-Time Head-to-Head Series

The 49ers hold the all-time series lead over the Rams, a product of their 1950s–1980s dominance before the Rams' move to St. Louis temporarily removed the rivalry's geographic intensity.

| Stat | 49ers | Rams |
|------|-------|------|
| All-time wins | 70 | 55 |
| Since Rams' LA return (2016–present) | 15 | 9 |
| Playoff meetings | 3 | 3 |
| Super Bowl meeting | 0 | 1 (Super Bowl LVI, 2022) |

**Since the Rams returned to Los Angeles**, the rivalry has been remarkably balanced with a slight 49ers edge. The 2022 NFC Championship game remains the most significant recent meeting — the Rams defeated San Francisco 20-17 in overtime at SoFi Stadium en route to winning Super Bowl LVI on their home field.

---

## Key Historical Matchups

### 2022 NFC Championship Game

The defining game of the modern rivalry era. The Rams trailed heading into the fourth quarter, but Matthew Stafford orchestrated a comeback, hitting Cooper Kupp on a critical third-down conversion before the game-winning touchdown. The 49ers led for most of the contest but couldn't close it out. The Rams went on to beat the Bengals in Super Bowl LVI.

### 2021 NFC Divisional Round

The previous playoff meeting went the other direction: San Francisco dominated Los Angeles 27-24 in a game where the 49ers' defense neutralized Stafford and the Rams' offense. Deebo Samuel was the offensive catalyst, rushing for a key touchdown. The 49ers advanced before losing to the Rams the following season in the Championship game.

### 2023 Season — Twin Thrillers

The 2023 regular season featured two games decided by a combined 7 points. The 49ers won both but not without drama — highlighting how competitive this division rivalry remains even when rosters change.

## Key Players in the Rivalry

### 49ers

**Brock Purdy** has emerged as the 49ers' franchise quarterback after being picked dead last in the 2022 draft (Mr. Irrelevant). Against the Rams, Purdy has shown composure and efficiency, though the 49ers' success in this matchup relies heavily on offensive coordinator Kyle Shanahan's scheme diversity.

**Christian McCaffrey** is the most versatile running back in the NFC and a nightmare for any defense. His ability to function as a receiver out of the backfield forces the Rams' linebackers to cover him in space — a mismatch they've struggled to solve.

**Nick Bosa** anchors a defensive line that has historically given the Rams' offensive line problems. Bosa's pass rush translates directly to pressures on whoever is playing quarterback for LA.

### Rams

**Matthew Stafford** remains the Rams' franchise quarterback in 2026, though durability concerns have been a recurring theme. When healthy and protected, Stafford's arm talent is elite for deep shot accuracy.

**Cooper Kupp** — when healthy — is Stafford's primary weapon and the player who comes up biggest in the most important moments. His slot-receiver route running and YAC ability make him the Rams' offensive engine.

**Puka Nacua** emerged as a legitimate receiving threat in 2023 (setting rookie receiving records) and has developed into a reliable second option alongside Kupp.

---

## What Drives This Rivalry

**Kyle Shanahan vs. Sean McVay:** Two of the most innovative offensive minds in the NFL face each other twice a year. Both coaches extensively share the same "outside zone" running scheme philosophy — McVay studied under Shanahan's father Mike. Each game features a chess match of play-calling adjustments and counter-adjustments.

**NFC West seeding implications:** In a division where the 49ers and Rams regularly battle for the top seed, their head-to-head meetings are often tiebreaker-defining. A 1-1 split in divisional play is the common outcome; winning the season series provides meaningful playoff seeding advantage.

**Fan intensity in LA:** The Rams' move to SoFi Stadium gave the rivalry a geographic flavor it lacked during the St. Louis era. 49ers fans, of whom there are many in Southern California, routinely fill a significant portion of SoFi Stadium for 49ers-Rams games — a point of ongoing tension.

---

## 2026 Preview

Both franchises enter 2026 with Super Bowl aspirations. The 49ers remain structurally excellent — elite receiver talent, a proven quarterback, and Shanahan's proven scheme advantage. The Rams have continued to build around Stafford while developing young talent through the draft.

**Key questions for 2026:**
- Can the Rams' offensive line protect Stafford against Bosa and the 49ers' pass rush?
- Is Deebo Samuel healthy enough to provide the run-pass-option threat the offense needs?
- Will the Rams' secondary be able to neutralize Aiyuk and McCaffrey in coverage?

Every 49ers-Rams game carries weight. Division title, wild card, home field, seeding — the stakes are almost always real when these two teams meet.
