# Liberty Tax Service: Prices, Locations, and How It Compares

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | August 4, 2026*

Liberty Tax Service is a national tax preparation franchise with more than 2,000 locations across the United States and Canada. It competes directly with H&R Block and Jackson Hewitt in the in-person tax preparation market. If you've ever seen someone dressed as the Statue of Liberty waving a sign near a strip mall in January, you've encountered Liberty Tax's signature marketing approach.

This guide covers what Liberty Tax charges, what services they offer, and how they compare to alternatives.

---

## What Liberty Tax Does

Liberty Tax prepares federal and state income tax returns through a network of franchise locations. Services include:

- **In-person tax preparation:** A tax preparer reviews your documents and files your return for you. This is Liberty Tax's core service.
- **Drop-off filing:** Leave your documents at a location, and a preparer files the return without requiring you to sit through the appointment
- **Virtual tax preparation:** Available in some markets — you share documents digitally and a preparer handles the rest
- **IRS audit support:** Access to representatives who can communicate with the IRS on your behalf if your return is audited
- **Refund advance loans:** Offered in partnership with banks — you receive a portion of your expected refund early (often the day you file), with fees built into the loan

---

## How Much Liberty Tax Costs

Liberty Tax doesn't publish a standard price list because pricing is set by individual franchise owners and varies by location, complexity, and state. However, based on available data and customer reports:

| Return Type | Estimated Cost Range |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Basic federal return (W-2 income, standard deduction) | $100–$200 |
| Federal + one state | $150–$270 |
| Itemized deductions | Add $50–$100 |
| Self-employment income (Schedule C) | $250–$400+ |
| Rental property (Schedule E) | Add $100–$150 per property |
| Multiple states | Add $50–$75 per state |

**Refund advance fees:** Liberty Tax offers refund advances through bank partnerships. These are technically no-interest loans, but they require purchasing tax preparation services at Liberty Tax's full price, and the advance is often for a portion of the expected refund, not the full amount.

**Key pricing reality:** Liberty Tax's in-person rates are not significantly different from H&R Block's published rates for comparable returns. For simple returns (W-2 income, no Schedule C, standard deduction), online software like TurboTax Free or IRS Free File is free or significantly cheaper.

---

## How to Find a Liberty Tax Location

Liberty Tax locations can be found at libertytax.com using the store locator. Most locations are open year-round, with extended hours during the January–April tax season.

**Seasonal availability:** Because Liberty Tax operates as a franchise, some locations may close during the off-season and reopen before the following filing season. Calling ahead before an out-of-season visit is recommended.

## Liberty Tax vs. H&R Block

| Feature | Liberty Tax | H&R Block |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Locations | 2,000+ | 9,000+ |
| In-person pricing | Similar (franchise-set) | Similar (published ranges) |
| Online software | Yes (basic) | Yes (more full-featured) |
| Audit support | Yes | Yes (Tax Pro Review available) |
| Year-round service | Most locations | Yes, all locations |
| Refund advance | Yes | Yes (Emerald Advance) |
| Preparer credentials | Varies by location | Varies; certified Tax Pros advertised |

**Bottom line:** H&R Block has significantly more locations, a more robust online filing product, and stronger brand recognition. For most people with access to H&R Block locations, it's the stronger choice between the two. Liberty Tax competes mainly on local presence and promotions.

---

## Liberty Tax vs. Jackson Hewitt

Jackson Hewitt is Liberty Tax's closest competitor in terms of positioning, pricing, and typical customer. Both serve working-class and middle-income filers who prefer in-person help. Key differences:

| Feature | Liberty Tax | Jackson Hewitt |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| Locations | 2,000+ | 5,500+ (many inside Walmart) |
| Walmart kiosks | No | Yes — significant differentiator |
| Pricing | Franchise-set | Franchise-set, similar range |
| Online filing | Yes | Yes |
| Refund advance | Yes | Yes (No Fee Refund Advance) |

Jackson Hewitt's partnership with Walmart locations is a meaningful advantage in accessibility — those locations have longer hours and are embedded in a high-traffic retail environment. If a Jackson Hewitt is more conveniently located for you, the in-store experience is comparable.

## Liberty Tax vs. TurboTax

This is the comparison that matters for most taxpayers:

| Feature | Liberty Tax (In-Person) | TurboTax (DIY) |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| Cost for simple return | ~$150–$200 | $0 (Free edition) to $129 |
| Cost for Schedule C | ~$300–$400 | $129–$219 (self-employed edition) |
| Guidance | Human preparer | Software walkthrough |
| Who does the work | Trained preparer | You |
| Error risk | Preparer's responsibility | Your responsibility |
| Best for | Complex returns, low tech comfort | Straightforward returns, tech-savvy filers |

If your return is straightforward — W-2 income, the standard deduction, maybe a child tax credit — TurboTax Free or IRS Free File handles it for free. The value of Liberty Tax's in-person service is most apparent for complex situations where human judgment matters: business income, multiple states, life changes like divorce or inheritance, or if you've had audit problems in the past.

## Who Should Use Liberty Tax

**Good fit:**
- Filers who prefer in-person help and want someone to review their documents
- Returns with complexity that exceeds comfortable DIY filing (multiple income sources, self-employment, rental property)
- People who don't trust online software or have had past issues with self-prepared returns
- Filers who need an in-person signature or verification for a loan application

**Not a good fit:**
- Simple W-2 returns with the standard deduction — online software is cheaper and faster
- People comfortable with tax software — TurboTax or H&R Block's online product delivers similar guidance at lower cost
- Filers needing maximum preparer credentials — a CPA or enrolled agent (available at many independent tax offices) provides higher-level expertise for complex situations

---

## Liberty Tax Pricing Tips

1. **Compare your Liberty Tax quote to H&R Block.** Call both for a quote on your specific return type before committing.
2. **Check for coupons.** Liberty Tax frequently runs promotions (percentage off, or fixed discounts) during the tax season. Their website and local franchise owners often advertise these.
3. **Consider the refund advance carefully.** The no-fee advance isn't free — it requires using Liberty Tax at full price. If you'd file with them anyway, it's a legitimate option to receive your refund faster. If you're using it to justify a higher-priced option, calculate whether the faster refund is worth the cost difference.
4. **Ask about off-season pricing.** If you're filing a prior-year return or an extension return, some locations discount off-season work.

## Quick Summary

- Liberty Tax is a national franchise with 2,000+ in-person locations; pricing is set by individual franchisees and varies
- Costs range from ~$150 for a simple return to $400+ for self-employment or complex returns
- H&R Block has more locations and a stronger online product; Jackson Hewitt has comparable in-person service, often inside Walmart stores
- TurboTax is significantly cheaper for simple returns; Liberty Tax's value is in human review for complex situations
- Always compare quotes from multiple preparers before committing
