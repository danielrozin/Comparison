# LegalZoom Contract Templates: What's Included, Costs, and Alternatives

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | August 6, 2026*

LegalZoom is one of the most recognized names in online legal services, and their contract templates are among their most commonly used products. For freelancers, small business owners, and landlords who need standard contract documents without paying attorney hourly rates, LegalZoom's templates offer a practical starting point — with important limitations to understand before you rely on one.

---

## What LegalZoom Contract Templates Cover

LegalZoom offers templates in several categories:

### Business Contracts
- **Independent Contractor Agreement:** Defines the relationship between a business and a freelancer or contractor — scope of work, payment terms, intellectual property ownership, non-disclosure provisions
- **Services Agreement:** General-purpose contract for service-based businesses
- **Partnership Agreement:** Defines terms between business partners — profit sharing, roles, decision-making authority, exit provisions
- **Operating Agreement (for LLCs):** Governs how an LLC is managed and owned
- **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA):** Protects confidential information shared between parties; both one-way (unilateral) and mutual NDA options are available
- **Purchase Order:** For businesses purchasing goods

### Employment and HR
- **Employment Offer Letter:** Formal offer document specifying title, salary, start date, and conditions of employment
- **Non-Compete Agreement:** Restricts employees or contractors from working for competitors after leaving
- **Employee Handbook:** Not technically a contract, but a customizable policy document

### Landlord-Tenant
- **Residential Lease Agreement:** State-specific lease templates for residential rentals
- **Month-to-Month Rental Agreement**
- **Lease Renewal Agreement**

### Personal and Estate
- **Last Will and Testament**
- **Living Will / Advance Healthcare Directive**
- **Power of Attorney**

## How LegalZoom Contract Templates Work

LegalZoom's contract template flow is straightforward:

1. Select the document type you need from their website
2. Answer a questionnaire that customizes the document to your situation (state, party names, key terms, payment amounts, etc.)
3. Review the generated document, edit directly if needed
4. Download in PDF or Word format

The Word format is particularly useful because it allows you to make further customizations that the questionnaire doesn't cover.

## What LegalZoom Contract Templates Cost

LegalZoom has shifted to a primarily subscription-based model for legal services. Standalone document purchases are still available in some cases, but the primary access model is:

**Legal Advantage Plans:**
| Plan | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost | Included |
|------|-------------|------------|---------|
| Business Essential | ~$7.99/mo | ~$95/yr | Legal document library access |
| Business Plus | ~$14.99/mo | ~$179/yr | Documents + 1 attorney consultation/year |
| Business Complete | ~$23.99/mo | ~$287/yr | Documents + 3 attorney consultations/year |

*Note: LegalZoom changes pricing regularly. Verify current rates on their website before subscribing.*

**Standalone documents (where available):** $19–$59 per document, depending on complexity.

For comparison:
- A freelance contract template from a legal template site like Docracy or Shake: $0–$15
- An NDA from Rocket Lawyer: Free for 7-day trial, then subscription-based
- A custom employment agreement from an attorney: $300–$1,500+

## LegalZoom vs. Rocket Lawyer

These two are the dominant players in online legal document templates:

| Feature | LegalZoom | Rocket Lawyer |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Document library | Large | Large |
| Attorney access | Yes (paid plans) | Yes (ask-a-lawyer feature) |
| Pricing model | Subscription | Subscription + free trial |
| Attorney consultation | Yes | Yes |
| Court filing services | Yes | Limited |
| Brand recognition | Higher | Slightly lower |
| Interface | Clear, questionnaire-driven | Similar |

Rocket Lawyer's free 7-day trial is a meaningful advantage if you need a single document and don't plan to use legal services regularly. LegalZoom's subscription has more value if you need ongoing access to multiple document types.

## When a LegalZoom Template Is Good Enough

Contract templates work well when:

**The transaction is routine and low-value:**
A standard freelance project agreement for a $2,000 web design project doesn't need custom attorney drafting. A template that covers scope, payment, revisions, and IP assignment is appropriate.

**Both parties have roughly equal negotiating power:**
Templates are neutral documents. When both parties are comfortable with the terms and neither has leverage to demand custom provisions, a template works.

**The template matches your state's law:**
LegalZoom's lease agreements, in particular, are state-specific and designed to comply with each state's landlord-tenant laws. This is a genuine advantage over generic templates.

**You're using it as a starting point you'll customize:**
The Word format allows you to add, remove, and modify provisions. A template plus your own additions is often more practical than a fully custom document for routine transactions.

## When You Need a Real Lawyer Instead

Templates are not appropriate when:

**Stakes are high:**
A contract for a $500,000 business acquisition, a multi-year commercial lease, or an employment agreement for a senior executive should be drafted by an attorney who understands your specific situation.

**The relationship is complex:**
Partnership agreements with asymmetric equity, vesting schedules, or complex IP arrangements; licensing agreements with royalty structures; joint venture agreements — these require customization that templates can't handle.

**You're in a dispute:**
If you're using a contract to resolve an existing disagreement, template language designed for new agreements often doesn't address the specific contested points. An attorney should draft or review anything you're signing in the context of a dispute.

**Industry-specific provisions matter:**
Healthcare contracts, music licensing agreements, software development agreements with complex IP chains — these industries have standard provisions that generic templates often miss.

**The other party has legal representation:**
If the counterparty has an attorney, you're at a negotiating disadvantage without one. Their attorney drafted the document to favor their client; you need someone reviewing it in your interest.

## Free Contract Template Alternatives

If you don't want to pay for LegalZoom's subscription, legitimate free options exist:

- **SCORE (score.org):** The Small Business Administration-backed mentorship network offers free contract templates and free mentor access to small business owners
- **Clerky and Stripe Atlas:** Offer standardized startup legal documents (SAFE notes, NDAs, equity agreements) free to users on their platforms
- **Docracy (docracy.com):** Community-sourced legal document repository; free downloads with user ratings
- **Your state bar's lawyer referral service:** Many state bars have a referral program connecting you to attorneys for a low-cost initial consultation ($50–$100)

## Quick Summary

- LegalZoom's contract templates cover freelance agreements, NDAs, leases, employment docs, and more
- Access is primarily through subscription plans ($8–$24/month); standalone documents cost $19–$59 each
- Templates are appropriate for routine, low-value transactions between parties comfortable with standard terms
- Complex deals, high-stakes agreements, or any situation involving an existing dispute warrant a real attorney
- Rocket Lawyer is the primary competitor; their free trial is useful for one-time document needs
