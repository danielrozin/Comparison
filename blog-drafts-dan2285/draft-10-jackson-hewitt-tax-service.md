# Jackson Hewitt Tax Service Review 2026

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | July 31, 2026*

Jackson Hewitt is the third-largest tax preparation company in the United States, with approximately 5,500 locations — a mix of standalone offices and kiosks inside Walmart stores. This review covers what Jackson Hewitt charges in 2026, what their Accuracy Guarantee covers, how to get their early refund advance, and how they compare to H&R Block and TurboTax.

---

## What Is Jackson Hewitt?

Jackson Hewitt Tax Service Inc. was founded in 1982 and operates as a franchise model. Most Jackson Hewitt offices are owned by franchisees rather than by the company itself, which means service quality and tax preparer expertise can vary significantly by location.

Key facts:
- ~5,500 US locations (Walmart kiosks + standalone offices)
- Operates seasonally (primarily January–April for most locations; some offer year-round service)
- Tax preparers are trained by Jackson Hewitt — they are not CPAs or enrolled agents (unless a specific location hires one)
- Also offers online DIY tax filing

---

## Jackson Hewitt Pricing (2026)

Jackson Hewitt doesn't publish standardized pricing on their website — fees vary by location and the complexity of your return. Based on typical customer reports:

| Tax Situation | Estimated Jackson Hewitt Fee |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| Simple federal + 1 state (W-2 only) | $100–$200 |
| Federal + state with itemized deductions | $150–$300 |
| Self-employment income (Schedule C) | $200–$400 |
| Multiple states | Add $50–$100 per additional state |
| Complex returns (investments, rental income) | $300–$500+ |

**Online DIY pricing (jacksonhewitt.com):**
- Simple federal return: $25 (W-2 income only)
- State return: $25 additional
- Complex returns: $35–$45 federal + $25 state

Jackson Hewitt's online software is less feature-rich than TurboTax or H&R Block but is cheaper for simple returns.

## Jackson Hewitt Accuracy Guarantee

Jackson Hewitt advertises a **"Guaranteed Biggest Refund"** and an **Accuracy Guarantee**. Here's what they actually cover:

**Accuracy Guarantee:** If an error made by Jackson Hewitt results in you paying penalties and interest to the IRS, they'll reimburse those penalties and interest. This does not cover the additional tax owed — only the penalty and interest components.

**Maximum Refund Guarantee:** If you discover that another preparer would have gotten you a larger refund, Jackson Hewitt says they'll refund your preparation fee. In practice, this requires showing an identical return prepared by a competitor.

**Important limitations:**
- You must notify Jackson Hewitt of the error within the time frame required to file an amended return (generally 3 years from the original return due date)
- The error must be Jackson Hewitt's mistake, not new information you provided after filing
- Accuracy guarantees don't help if the tax law itself is the issue — only mathematical or preparatory errors are covered

H&R Block, Liberty Tax, and TurboTax all offer similar guarantees. They're meaningful protection but not as comprehensive as using a CPA who carries professional liability insurance (E&O insurance).

## Early Refund Advance (No-Fee Loan)

Jackson Hewitt offers **early tax refund advances** — interest-free loans of $500–$7,000 that are paid back automatically when your IRS refund arrives.

**2026 details:**
- Advance amounts: $500, $1,000, $1,500, $2,500, $3,500, $5,000, or $7,000
- No interest, no fees on the advance itself (preparation fees still apply)
- Available January–February 2026 at participating locations
- Deposited to a Jackson Hewitt debit card (MetaBank card) typically within 24 hours of approval
- Requires filing your taxes in-person at a Jackson Hewitt location (not available for DIY online filers)

**The catch:** The advance is only available to customers using Jackson Hewitt's in-person tax preparation — which means you're paying the preparation fee ($100–$400+) to access the advance. If you qualify for IRS Free File or free tax prep (VITA), you'd be paying preparation fees you don't need to pay in exchange for getting your refund a week earlier.

For people who genuinely need cash and whose refund will cover the advance, it's better than a payday loan. But compare against whether you could wait 7–21 days for IRS direct deposit instead.

## When to Use Jackson Hewitt

**Good fit:**
- You live near a Walmart with a Jackson Hewitt kiosk and want in-person help
- Your tax situation is simple (W-2 income, standard deduction)
- You want the refund advance and can't wait for IRS direct deposit
- Price is a primary concern and online filing intimidates you

**Not a good fit:**
- Self-employed with complex deductions (better with a CPA or TurboTax Self-Employed)
- Have investment income, rental properties, or foreign accounts
- Are comfortable filing online (Free File or FreeTaxUSA is dramatically cheaper)
- Need year-round tax support (Jackson Hewitt is largely seasonal)

---

## Jackson Hewitt Alternatives

**For free federal filing:** IRS Free File through IRS.gov (AGI ≤$79,000)

**For cheap complex returns:** FreeTaxUSA ($0 federal + $14.99 state) handles self-employment, rental income, and investment sales — the same situations Jackson Hewitt charges $300+ for

**For in-person help at similar prices:** H&R Block, with more locations and slightly stronger national training standards

**For best in-person expertise:** A local CPA or enrolled agent (EA) — expect $250–$600 for a typical personal return, but the preparer has professional licensing and carries E&O insurance

**For truly free help:** VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance) — IRS-certified volunteers at community locations for incomes under ~$67,000

## Quick Summary

- Jackson Hewitt has ~5,500 locations (mostly Walmart kiosks) and is best suited to simple in-person returns
- In-person fees typically run $100–$300 for W-2-only returns; complex returns $300–$500+
- Online DIY is cheaper ($25 fed + $25 state) but the software is less robust than TurboTax or H&R Block
- Refund Advance (up to $7,000 interest-free) is available January–February for in-person filers only
- Accuracy Guarantee covers IRS penalties and interest from their errors — not the additional tax itself
- For simple returns under $79K AGI: Free File options (FreeTaxUSA, Cash App Taxes) are a better deal
