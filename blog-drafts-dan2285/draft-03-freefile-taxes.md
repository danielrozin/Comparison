# IRS Free File: How to File Your Taxes for Free in 2026

*By Daniel Rozin | A Versus B | July 24, 2026*

**IRS Free File** is a partnership between the IRS and private tax software companies that allows taxpayers with adjusted gross income (AGI) of **$79,000 or less** to file their federal return at no cost. In 2026, approximately 70% of US taxpayers qualify — yet fewer than 3% actually use the program. If you've been paying $30–$120 to file your federal taxes, there's a good chance you didn't have to.

---

## What Is IRS Free File?

Free File is not a single piece of software — it's a program that offers two pathways:

### 1. Free File Guided Software
Tax software from commercial providers (including TaxAct, FreeTaxUSA, and others) at no cost for eligible taxpayers. The software asks you questions and fills in the forms automatically — the same experience as paid software, just free.

**Eligibility:** AGI of $79,000 or below for the 2025 tax year (returns filed in 2026).

**Available through:** IRS.gov/freefile — do NOT search for "free file" on Google and click the first result. Accessing Free File correctly requires going through the IRS website, which directs you to the participating providers. Using a provider's website directly may charge you.

### 2. Free File Fillable Forms
Electronic versions of IRS tax forms you fill out yourself — no guided interview, no calculations. Available to all taxpayers regardless of income.

**Who it's for:** Taxpayers comfortable with tax math who know exactly what forms they need and prefer to complete them manually.

---

## Who Qualifies for Free File Software?

For the 2025 tax year (returns filed in 2026):

- **AGI $79,000 or below:** Eligible for guided free software
- **AGI above $79,000:** Eligible for Free File Fillable Forms only
- **Military members:** Free federal and state filing through MilTax (separate program) regardless of income

Each participating provider sets their own additional eligibility criteria within the $79,000 AGI cap. Some limit by age, state, or specific income types. The IRS's Free File lookup tool matches you with eligible providers after you answer a few basic questions.

---

## Best Free File Options for 2026

### FreeTaxUSA
The most recommended free option among experienced filers. FreeTaxUSA handles nearly every tax situation for federal returns — including self-employment income (Schedule C), rental income (Schedule E), investment sales, and itemized deductions. Federal is 100% free; state returns cost $14.99.

### TaxAct Free File
TaxAct's Free File edition supports W-2 income, unemployment, simple interest and dividend income. More limited than FreeTaxUSA for complex returns. Some users report TaxAct attempting to upsell aggressively.

### Cash App Taxes (formerly Credit Karma Tax)
Completely free for both federal and state returns with no income limit. Handles most common tax situations including self-employment, but has had some limitations with complex business situations. Solid option for straightforward returns.

### IRS Direct File (limited states)
The IRS's own free filing tool, launched in 2024 and expanded in 2025–2026. Available in a growing number of states. Handles W-2 income, Social Security, interest income, and the Child Tax Credit directly. If your situation is simple and you live in a participating state, Direct File is the purest no-catch option.

## How to Access Free File Without Getting Charged

The most common mistake: searching "TurboTax free file" or similar terms and landing on the paid version of a commercial product.

**The correct path:**
1. Go to **IRS.gov** → Click "File" → Click "Free File"
2. Click "Use Free Guided Tax Software"
3. Click "Browse All Offers" or use the "Help Me Find Offers" tool
4. Select a provider and click through from the IRS website directly

Some providers offer a "free" tier on their own website that is NOT the same as the Free File version — it includes income limits, form restrictions, or charges for features included in the Free File version.

**Tip:** Bookmark the direct IRS Free File landing page: **irs.gov/freefile**

---

## State Tax Returns Are Separate

Free File covers **federal returns only** (except Cash App Taxes, which includes free state filing, and Direct File, which handles participating state returns directly).

For state returns, your options are:
- **Cash App Taxes:** Free state return for all states where they operate
- **FreeTaxUSA:** $14.99 per state return
- **Your state's own free filing program:** Many states have their own free e-filing for simple returns. Check your state's department of revenue website.
- **VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance):** Free in-person preparation for incomes under ~$67,000 (run by IRS-certified volunteers)

## What Free File Cannot Handle

Free File works well for the majority of taxpayers, but has limitations:

| Tax Situation | Free File? | Better Option |
|---------------|------------|---------------|
| W-2 income only | ✅ Excellent | Any provider |
| Self-employment income (Sch C) | ✅ FreeTaxUSA handles this | FreeTaxUSA |
| Rental income (Sch E) | ✅ FreeTaxUSA handles this | FreeTaxUSA |
| Investment sales (Sch D) | ✅ Depends on provider | FreeTaxUSA or Cash App |
| Foreign income or FBAR | ⚠️ Limited | May need a paid preparer |
| Complex business entities (S-corp, partnership) | ❌ | CPA or paid preparer |
| Multi-state returns | ⚠️ Limited | Paid software or CPA |

---

## VITA and TCE: Free In-Person Help

If you prefer in-person assistance:

**VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance):** Free tax preparation from IRS-certified volunteers for taxpayers with incomes around $67,000 or less, people with disabilities, and those with limited English proficiency.

**TCE (Tax Counseling for the Elderly):** Free preparation for taxpayers 60 and older, specializing in pension and retirement-related tax questions.

Find a VITA/TCE site at **irs.gov/vita** or call 1-800-906-9887.

## Filing Deadlines for 2026

| Deadline | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **April 15, 2026** | Federal return due for 2025 tax year; last day for 2025 IRA/Roth IRA contributions |
| **October 15, 2026** | Extended deadline (if Form 4868 filed by April 15) |
| **October 15, 2026** | Last day for self-employed to fund a SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) for 2025 (with extension) |

An extension gives you more time to **file**, not more time to **pay**. If you owe taxes, interest and penalties accrue from April 15 even with an extension.

## Quick Summary

- IRS Free File allows free federal filing for AGIs at or below $79,000
- Use FreeTaxUSA for complex returns (self-employment, rental income, investment sales)
- Use Cash App Taxes if you want both federal and state free
- Use IRS Direct File if available in your state for a completely IRS-run experience
- Always access Free File through **IRS.gov** — not through commercial software websites
- State returns are usually separate ($0–$15 depending on the provider)
