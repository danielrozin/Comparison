## Budget Reality Check

LinkedIn's platform minimum is $10/day for most campaign objectives. At $3/day, the strategy must use one of these approaches:

1. **Manual bidding with maximum delivery** -- set a $3/day campaign budget and let LinkedIn optimize within that constraint. Some objectives (e.g., website visits) allow budgets below $10/day with manual CPC bidding.
2. **Thought Leader Ads (TLA) via personal profile posts** -- boost organic posts as ads. TLAs have 1.7x higher CTR and CPC as low as $2.29-$4.14 (vs $13.23 standard). This is the recommended path at this budget.
3. **Rotate concepts weekly, not simultaneously** -- run one concept at a time for 7-10 days rather than splitting $3/day across multiple ads.

**Expected reach at $3/day:** With LinkedIn CPMs of $31-$38, expect roughly 80-100 impressions/day. Over 30 days, that is approximately 2,400-3,000 impressions/month. Every impression must count -- targeting precision is everything.

## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Marketing Wall
**Hypothesis:** Self-published authors on LinkedIn frequently post about the struggle of book marketing after launch. Tapping into this shared frustration creates immediate recognition and stops the scroll.
**Primary Message:** You spent months writing your book. You shouldn't spend months learning how to market it.
**Tone:** Direct 8/10, empowering 7/10, professional 8/10. Acknowledge the pain without being condescending -- speak to authors as capable professionals who simply lack the right tool.
**Visual Direction:** UGC-style video with a relatable author figure expressing the frustration of staring at ad platforms, followed by the simplicity reveal. Static companion: dark background (#000000) with a stark split -- one side showing a tangled mess of marketing tasks, the other showing a single book link input field glowing in emerald (#10B981).
**Target Platforms:** LinkedIn -- this pain point resonates deeply with the author/publisher community that actively networks on LinkedIn. Best as a Thought Leader Ad from a founder or team member's personal post.
**CTA:** Try For Free
**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- pain point recognition among target audience.

### Concept 2: The 2-Minute Campaign
**Hypothesis:** Demonstrating the speed and simplicity of the product (paste a link, get a full campaign in under 2 minutes) directly counters the audience's belief that running ads requires design skills, video editing, or agency fees.
**Primary Message:** Paste your book link. Get UGC videos, static ads, and Google campaigns -- in 2 minutes.
**Tone:** Innovative 9/10, direct 8/10, bold 7/10. The tone should convey confidence in the product's capability without hype. Let the speed speak for itself.
**Visual Direction:** Screen-recording-style UGC video showing the actual workflow: paste a link, watch the AI generate assets in real time. The "before/after" of an empty dashboard vs. a full campaign suite is the visual hook. Static companion: dark (#000000) background with a clean timeline graphic -- a single indigo (#4F46E5) line from "Book Link" to "Full Campaign" with a timestamp overlay showing "1:47."
**Target Platforms:** LinkedIn -- product demonstrations perform well on LinkedIn, especially for SaaS. Ideal as a sponsored video post or document ad showing the step-by-step.
**CTA:** Try For Free
**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- transformation/demo angle to convert interest into trial signups.

**Hypothesis:** Authors on LinkedIn are aware that book marketing agencies charge $1,000-$5,000/month. Framing ADGPT as the cost comparison -- $29/month vs. $3,000/month for similar output -- creates a compelling ROI narrative backed by social proof.

Static companion: dark background (#000000) with a bold cost comparison -- agency price crossed out in muted gray, ADGPT price highlighted in emerald (#10B981), with the star rating and campaign count as supporting proof points.

**Addresses:** General brand awareness -- ROI/social proof angle to convert price-sensitive prospects.
